Block 1 · Minutes 0–50
The Basic Tool: A Good Translation
기본 도구: 좋은 번역본
⏱ 0:00 — The Linguist's Dilemma (10 min)
Let me begin with a confession that may disturb you — and should. Every time you open your English Bible and read it, you are not reading the Bible. You are reading a translation. And every translation is already an interpretation. The sixty-six books were composed in three languages: Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek. Unless you have studied all three, you have never read a single verse in its original form.
한 가지 고백으로 시작하겠습니다. 영어 성경을 펼칠 때마다, 당신은 번역본을 읽는 것입니다. 모든 번역은 이미 해석입니다. 66권은 히브리어, 아람어, 그리스어로 기록되었습니다. 세 언어를 공부하지 않았다면, 원래 형태로 한 구절도 읽은 적이 없습니다.
I. The Three Languages of the Biblical Text
성경의 세 언어
Biblical Hebrew (OT, with Aramaic in Ezra/Daniel) is a language of striking concreteness — rooted in images of movement, body, and land. Its verbs are the backbone of the sentence. Koine Greek (NT) is the common Greek of the marketplace, spread by Alexander's conquests: simpler, more flexible, the Greek of ordinary people writing with apostolic urgency. Each language carries semantic ranges that do not map perfectly onto English. Translation requires interpretation. The question is not whether to interpret — it is whether to do so well or poorly.
성경 히브리어(구약)는 움직임, 신체, 땅의 이미지에 뿌리를 둔 구체성의 언어입니다. 동사가 문장의 뼈대입니다. 코이네 그리스어(신약)는 시장의 일상 그리스어로, 사도적 긴박감을 가진 언어입니다. 번역은 해석을 필요로 합니다. 문제는 해석할 것인지가 아니라 — 잘 할 것인지입니다.
II. The 1 Corinthians 7:36 Case Study
번역 비교 사례: 고린도전서 7:36
⏱ 0:20 — Translation Philosophy (15 min)
Fee and Stuart use 1 Corinthians 7:36 as their primary case study. Four translations render this verse with four different social scenarios — a man and his fiancée? a father and his daughter? a celibate couple? The Greek is genuinely ambiguous — but Paul was not. He wrote to a community that already knew the situation. Where translations agree, you are on solid ground. Where they diverge, a genuine interpretive difficulty is surfaced — your invitation to dig deeper.
NKJV
"If any man thinks that he is behaving improperly toward his virgin…"
NASB
"If any man thinks that he is acting unbecomingly toward his virgin daughter…"
NIV
"If anyone is worried that he might not be acting honorably toward the virgin he is engaged to…"
NEB
"If a man has a partner in celibacy and feels that he is not behaving properly towards her…"
Where translations agree, you are on solid ground. Where they diverge, you are looking at a genuine difficulty in the text — and that divergence is your invitation to dig deeper.
— Fee & Stuart
III. A Taxonomy of English Translations
번역 철학의 스펙트럼
Formal Equivalence (Word-for-Word)
Dynamic Equivalence (Thought-for-Thought)
Preserves word order and grammatical structures. NASB, ESV, NKJV. Best for close study. Risk: idioms translated word-for-word can mislead.
Renders the meaning in natural English. NIV, NLT. More readable. Risk: the translator's interpretation becomes embedded — e.g. NIV renders σάρξ as 'sinful nature' (one choice among several).
Paraphrase (The Message, Living Bible): expansive, interpretive retellings. Valuable devotionally — never as a primary study text. Practical wisdom: NASB/ESV for formal study; NIV/CSB for readable checking; compare all on difficult passages.
의역 (메시지 성경): 광범위한 해석적 재표현입니다. 묵상에는 가치 있지만 — 주요 연구 텍스트로 사용하지 마십시오. 실용적 지혜: NASB/ESV를 형식적 연구에; NIV/CSB를 가독성 확인에 사용하고, 어려운 구절에서는 모두 비교하십시오.
IV. Korean Linguistic Bridge
한국어 언어적 가교
Korean translations — 개역개정, 새번역, 현대인의 성경 — each carry their own translation philosophies. Korean's verb-final structure aligns more naturally with Hebrew and Greek in some respects than English. Korean honorific levels require constant decisions about relational posture that English's single 'you' never faces. A bilingual reader moving between English and Korean has a richer interpretive resource than one confined to one tradition.
Block 2 · Minutes 60–110
The Hermeneutical Spiral
해석학적 나선: 이해의 형태
⏱ 1:00 — The Shape of Understanding (12 min)
I. The Gold Coin Metaphor
황금 동전의 비유
Osborne opens his book with a vivid image: gold coins have been discovered on the church's back lot — and soundings show the number increasing with every foot of depth, down to 200 feet. How quickly would you start digging? This is Bible study. The deeper you go, the greater the rewards.
오스본은 생생한 이미지로 시작합니다: 교회 뒤뜰에 황금 동전이 발견되었고 — 지하 200피트까지 매 피트마다 동전 수가 늘어납니다. 더 깊이 들어갈수록, 보상은 더 커집니다.
Biblical interpretation is not a closed circle but a spiral. The hermeneutical circle (Schleiermacher, Gadamer) says the whole is understood through its parts and parts through the whole — true, but a closed circle implies interpretation merely loops back to confirm what we already believe. Osborne's spiral moves: it ascends toward the text's intended meaning. And it is cone-shaped — it converges.
성경 해석은 닫힌 원이 아니라 나선형입니다. 닫힌 원은 해석이 이미 믿는 것을 확인하는 루프를 돈다는 것을 암시합니다. 오스본의 나선형은 움직입니다: 텍스트의 의도된 의미를 향해 상승하며 수렴합니다.
It is not a closed circle that can never detect the true meaning but [I am] spiraling nearer and nearer to the text's intended meaning as I refine my hypotheses and allow the text to continue to challenge and correct those alternative interpretations.
— Grant Osborne
⏱ 1:12 — Science, Art, Spirit (8 min)
II. Three Dimensions of Hermeneutics
과학, 예술, 영적 행위
A science — providing logical, orderly classification of interpretive laws. It makes it possible to say some readings are better than others.
An art — an acquired skill demanding imagination and practice. The art develops through thousands of hours of encounter with the text.
A spiritual act — uniquely dependent on the illuminating work of the Holy Spirit. Human effort is necessary and never sufficient. Illumination presupposes rigorous study — it does not replace it.
⏱ 1:20 — The Four Distances (10 min)
III. The Four Distances (Klein, Blomberg, Hubbard)
네 가지 거리
Paul Ricoeur speaks of the distanciation gap between the biblical world and contemporary readers. Klein, Blomberg, and Hubbard identify four areas every interpreter must reckon with:
폴 리쾨르는 성경 세계와 현대 독자 사이의 거리화 간격에 대해 말합니다. 클라인, 블롬버그, 허바드는 모든 해석자가 고려해야 할 네 가지 거리 영역을 식별합니다: 시간, 문화, 지리, 언어.
Time
Culture
The biblical books span ~1,500 years of composition. We are two millennia from the most recent NT writings.
Social structures, economic systems, legal frameworks of the ancient world are profoundly different from ours.
Geography
Language
Places are constitutive elements of meaning, not mere backdrop. Roman occupation means something different to those who have never experienced subjugation.
Hebrew and Greek are living language systems with their own logic and idioms — not codes to decode by consulting a lexicon.
⏱ 1:30 — Hirsch's Distinction (8 min)
IV. Meaning vs. Significance — Hirsch
의미와 의의 — 허쉬의 구분
Meaning
Significance
The author's intended content — singular, stable, determined at the moment of writing. What Paul meant by Romans 6:23 in 58 A.D. is the same whether read in Rome or Seoul today.
The relationship between that fixed meaning and a later reader's situation. Different readers, different significance — but the same meaning.
A text cannot mean what it could never have meant for its original readers/hearers. The true meaning of the biblical text for us is what God originally intended it to mean when it was first spoken or written.
— Fee & Stuart
⏱ 1:38 — Context (22 min)
V. Context — The Most Important Word
문맥 — 해석학에서 가장 중요한 단어
Osborne opens Chapter 1 with a confession: if a student falls half asleep and misses a question, there is a 50% chance of being correct by answering 'context.' Words mean things in sentences, paragraphs, books, and historical settings — never in isolation.
오스본은 1장을 고백으로 시작합니다: 학생이 반쯤 졸다가 질문을 놓쳤다면, '문맥'이라고 답하기만 해도 맞을 확률이 50%입니다. 단어들은 문장, 단락, 책, 역사적 배경 안에서 의미를 가집니다 — 결코 고립되어서는 아닙니다.
A. Historical Context
Knowing that Amos was a shepherd from Tekoa, delivering oracles to the prosperous but structurally unjust northern kingdom during Jeroboam II's reign, transforms 'those who trample the poor' from abstract moral platitude into surgical diagnosis.
아모스가 드고아 출신 목자로서 여로보암 2세 통치 기간에 불의한 북왕국에 신탁을 전했다는 것을 알면, '가난한 자를 짓밟는 자들'이 추상적 도덕 교훈에서 정밀한 사회 진단으로 변모합니다.
B. Logical Context (Concentric Circles)
Logical context moves from immediate sentences outward to section, book, canon. The immediate context is the final arbiter for word meaning. The discipline of charting a book — reading it whole before studying any individual passage — prevents the most common exegetical errors.
논리적 문맥은 즉각적 문장에서 섹션, 책, 정경으로 이동합니다. 즉각적 문맥이 단어 의미의 최종 중재자입니다. 책 도표 작성의 훈련 — 개별 구절을 공부하기 전에 전체를 읽는 것 — 이 가장 흔한 주해 오류를 방지합니다.
Block 3 · Minutes 120–170
Grammar, Textual Criticism, and the Surface of the Text
문법, 본문 비평, 그리고 텍스트의 표면
⏱ 2:00 — Exegesis vs. Eisegesis (5 min)
I. Exegesis and Eisegesis
주해와 삽입 해석
Exegesis (ἐξήγησις)
Eisegesis (εἰσ-)
"To lead out of." Reading meaning out of the text — drawing out what the author put there.
"Into." Reading meaning into the text — projecting your assumptions, tradition, or predetermined conclusion onto it.
Every interpreter commits eisegesis — constantly, unavoidably. Good hermeneutics does not eliminate these filters (impossible) but names them, holds them loosely, and allows the text to interrogate and reform them. This is why the spiral is the right metaphor.
모든 해석자는 끊임없이, 불가피하게 삽입 해석을 범합니다. 좋은 해석학은 이 필터들을 제거하는 것이 아니라 그것들을 명명하고, 텍스트가 그것들을 심문하고 개혁하도록 허용합니다. 이것이 나선형이 올바른 은유인 이유입니다.
⏱ 2:05 — Textual Criticism (20 min)
II. Textual Criticism: What Exactly Is the Text?
본문 비평: 텍스트는 정확히 무엇인가?
We do not possess the original autographs. We have copies of copies — over 5,700 Greek NT manuscripts. The OT tradition includes the Masoretic Text (vocalized 6th–10th c. A.D.), the Septuagint/LXX (~3rd–1st c. B.C., translated from pre-Masoretic Hebrew), the Samaritan Pentateuch, and the Dead Sea Scrolls (discovered 1947 — pushing our oldest Hebrew manuscripts back ~1,000 years and largely confirming the Masoretic tradition).
우리는 원본을 소유하지 않습니다. 사본의 사본들만 있습니다 — 신약 5,700개 이상의 그리스어 사본. 구약 전통에는 마소라 텍스트, 칠십인역(LXX), 사마리아 오경, 사해 사본(1947년)이 있습니다.
Case Study: Mark 16:9–20
The longer ending of Mark (vv. 9–20) appears in the KJV but is absent or bracketed in virtually all modern translations. The two oldest and most reliable manuscripts — Codex Sinaiticus and Codex Vaticanus — end at 16:8: "for they were afraid." The vocabulary and style of vv. 9–20 differ noticeably from Mark's language elsewhere. A later editor appears to have compiled resurrection-appearance traditions and attached them to Mark's abrupt ending. Responsible response: acknowledge it honestly; note that no doctrine depends solely on these verses.
마가복음의 긴 결말(9-20절)은 KJV에 있지만 현대 번역본에는 없거나 괄호로 묶여 있습니다. 가장 오래된 두 사본 — 시나이 사본과 바티칸 사본 — 은 16:8에서 끝납니다: "그들이 두려워하였기 때문이라." 책임 있는 응답: 정직하게 인정하고, 어떤 교리도 이 구절에만 의존하지 않음을 언급하십시오.
External criteria: How early is the manuscript? What is the geographical distribution? Is the reading found in multiple independent families?
Internal criteria: Which reading is more difficult? (Scribes simplified, not complicated.) Which is shorter? Which fits the author's style?
⏱ 2:25 — Grammar and Meaning (25 min)
III. Grammatical Analysis
문법 분석
Grammar is the architectural blueprint of communication. Word meaning is not self-contained: words mean things only in grammatical context.
Case Study: Greek Present Tense in 1 John 3:9
The Greek tense system encodes not just time but verbal aspect — how the speaker presents the action. The present tense indicates ongoing, habitual action. In 1 John 3:9, the NIV's "continue to sin" and "go on sinning" reflect Greek present forms indicating a characteristic lifestyle — not occasional sins. John is not saying believers never sin; he is saying that sinning as a characteristic pattern is incompatible with being born of God. This grammatical distinction changes the entire interpretation.
그리스어 시제 체계는 시간만이 아니라 언어적 상(verbal aspect)을 인코딩합니다. 요한일서 3:9의 현재 시제는 특징적인 생활 방식을 나타냅니다 — 가끔 있는 죄가 아닙니다. 이 문법적 구분이 해석 전체를 바꿉니다.
Case Study: The Philippians 2:6 Participle
The participle ὑπάρχων (hyparchon) in Philippians 2:6 can be translated three ways — each with significant Christological implications:
Concessive
"Although he was in the form of God…" — divine nature as backdrop to self-emptying.
Causal
"Because he was in the form of God…" — divinity as motivation rather than contrast.
Temporal
"When he was in the form of God…" — locating the action in time relative to the incarnation.
⏱ 2:50 — Integration (10 min)
IV. Integration: Grammar, Translation, Formation
통합: 세 블록의 하나된 과제
Translation, context, and grammar are not three separate topics — they are three angles on the same task: understanding what God said in Scripture and how that word speaks into our world. In the WHERE–WHO–WHAT framework: hermeneutics locates you (WHERE), reconstitutes you as an interpreter within community (WHO), and shapes your vocation — not merely to understand Scripture but to allow Scripture to understand, reform, and locate you (WHAT).
번역, 문맥, 문법은 동일한 과제의 세 가지 각도입니다. WHERE–WHO–WHAT 틀에서: 해석학은 당신을 위치시키고(WHERE), 공동체 안의 해석자로 재구성하며(WHO), 소명을 형성합니다(WHAT) — 성경이 당신을 이해하고, 개혁하고, 위치시키도록 허용하는 것.
O God of word and wisdom, you have spoken in many ways and by many instruments. Teach us to receive what you have given — not with careless haste, but with the patient attention of those who know that to understand your word is to encounter you.
— Closing Benediction

Discussion Questions
Q1
Fee and Stuart argue that using only one Bible translation commits you to the exegetical decisions of that committee. How does this challenge the way you have read the Bible in the past?
피와 스튜어트는 하나의 번역본만 사용하는 것은 그 위원회의 주해적 결정에 자신을 맡기는 것이라고 주장합니다. 이것이 과거에 당신이 성경을 읽어온 방식에 어떤 도전을 줍니까?
Q2

오스본은 해석학을 동시에 과학, 예술, 영적 행위로 묘사합니다. 당신의 실천에서 어느 차원이 가장 발전해 있습니까? 어느 것이 가장 덜 발전해 있습니까?
Q3
The 'problem of distance' (time, culture, geography, language) is real. But Osborne argues it is bridgeable. What resources do you have right now for bridging that gap?
'거리의 문제'(시간, 문화, 지리, 언어)는 실재합니다. 그러나 오스본은 그것이 연결 가능하다고 주장합니다. 지금 당신이 그 간격을 연결하기 위해 가진 자원은 무엇입니까?
Q4
Hirsch's distinction between meaning and significance is central to Osborne's project. How does it change the way you think about 'applying' a biblical text?
허쉬의 의미와 의의의 구분은 오스본 프로젝트의 핵심입니다. 이것이 성경 텍스트를 '적용하는' 것에 대해 당신이 생각하는 방식을 어떻게 바꿉니까?
Q5
For Korean students: How does reading in 개역개정 or 새번역 alongside English change or deepen your interpretation? Give a specific example.
한국어 학생들에게: 영어와 함께 개역개정이나 새번역으로 읽는 것이 어떻게 해석을 변화시키거나 심화시킵니까? 구체적인 예를 들어 설명하십시오.
Q6
Osborne insists charting an entire book before studying individual passages is invaluable. Have you done this? If not, which book would you start with — and why?
오스본은 개별 구절을 공부하기 전에 책 전체를 도표화하는 것이 매우 중요하다고 강조합니다. 이것을 해본 적 있습니까? 없다면, 어떤 책으로 시작하겠습니까 — 그리고 왜입니까?
